Sonification Resources

I'm rather new to sonification's mysteries, pleasures, uses, and frustrations, but it's been around for awhile, with people exploring all sorts of aspects and applications for it. Here's a few other researchers and sonifiers that have inspired me and whose work helped me along the way so far:

Thomas Herrman provides a good introduction to the field, and some of his work sonifying motion.

Georgia Tech has a interdisciplinary Sonification Lab where they research sonification with an emphasis on using it to help people with vision disabilities navigate, learn math, and even explore and experience an aquarium.

LHCsound is a group of physicists who are exploring Large Hadron Collider data via sound.

John Flowers of the University of Nebraska has a nice summary of some of the potential and pitfalls of using sonification to explore datasets.
